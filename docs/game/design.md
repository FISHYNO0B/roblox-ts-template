# Design

> Replace this file when the template is forked into a real game. The headings below are the structure to keep — the content is placeholder.

The **what** and **why** of the game. Pitch, pillars, references, and what is explicitly *not* in scope. Numbers belong in [balancing.md](balancing.md), not here.

## Pitch

_One or two sentences. The elevator version. What does the player do, and why is it satisfying?_

> Example: "A reseller-tycoon where you flip items for profit, place workers that generate items 24/7, and grow an office empire on your private plot."

## Genre / references

- **Genre:** _e.g. simulator, tycoon, obby, RPG, idle…_
- **References:** _2–4 existing Roblox or non-Roblox games this draws from. Include what specifically you're borrowing (loop, art, pacing, monetization)._

## Player fantasy

_One paragraph. What does the player feel like they are, in the game's world? "I am a small-time reseller building an empire" / "I am a wizard mastering elemental magic". This guides every UX call later._

## Design pillars

Three to five things every gameplay decision must serve. If a feature can't be defended against the pillars, it doesn't ship.

1. _Pillar one — short rationale._
2. _Pillar two — short rationale._
3. _Pillar three — short rationale._

## Non-goals

Things this game is deliberately **not**. Listing them up front prevents scope creep and makes "no" decisions cheaper later.

- _e.g. No PvP._
- _e.g. No trading between players._
- _e.g. No rebirth / progression resets._
- _e.g. No crafting._

## Core loop

_The 30-second-to-2-minute loop the player repeats. Bullet steps:_

1. _Player does X._
2. _Which gives them Y._
3. _Which they spend on Z._
4. _Which unlocks more of X._
